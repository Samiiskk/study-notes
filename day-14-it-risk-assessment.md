## What is IT Risk?
Potential for loss, damage, or disruption to information systems,
services, or data — caused by cyber attacks, system failures,
human error, or third-party risks.

## Four Categories of IT Risk

| Category | Cause | Example |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Operational | Internal system/process/personnel failures | System outages, human errors |
| Security | Unauthorized access, breaches, cyber attacks | Data theft, ransomware |
| Compliance | Failing to follow laws/regulations | GDPR/HIPAA violations and fines |
| Strategic | IT initiatives misaligned with business goals | Failed e-commerce platform launch |

### Case Study: Sony PlayStation Network Breach (2011)
Shows how risk categories interconnect:
- **Security risk** — hacking and data theft
- **Operational risk** — service downtime
- **Compliance risk** — legal penalties

**How they connect:** A security breach often causes operational
downtime (systems shut down to contain it) AND compliance risk
(unauthorized data access violates regulations).

## Risk Management Frameworks

### ISO 31000
- Versatile across industries
- Systematic risk identification, assessment, treatment, monitoring
- Flexible — aligns with organizational strategy

### NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)
- Common in US government and private sector
- Categorize systems by risk impact
- Select and implement security controls
- Authorize systems, continuous monitoring
- Suited for strict compliance environments (govt, healthcare)

---

## Risk Register

A tool for tracking and managing risks systematically.

### Key Components
- Risk description
- Likelihood of occurrence
- Impact severity
- Mitigation plan
- Designated risk owner

### Case Study: Financial Institution
Used risk register for mobile banking platform — tracked cyber
attacks, data breaches, service outages. Prioritized high
likelihood + high impact risks, assigned ownership, implemented MFA.

### Best Practices
- Regularly update as risks evolve
- Use clear, jargon-free descriptions
- Prioritize high-impact risks
- Assign clear ownership and accountability
- Don't overcomplicate with excessive detail

## Integrating Business Processes into Risk Assessment

**Steps:**
1. Identify and prioritize core business processes
2. Map potential risks to each process
3. Assess likelihood and impact of each risk
4. Align risk strategies with business priorities

**Case study:** Retail company integrated supply chain risks
(supplier, transportation, IT systems) — reduced disruptions.

## Risk Scenarios

**What they are:** Detailed narratives describing specific threats,
their causes, consequences, and likelihood.

**Components:**
- Triggering event
- Actors involved
- Impact
- Existing controls

**Prioritization:** Focus on high-impact, high-likelihood risks first.
Use cross-functional teams and tabletop exercises.

## Current Cybersecurity Threat Landscape

### Key Trends
- Digital transformation expanded attack surfaces (cloud, remote work, IoT)
- Increased ransomware attacks
- Nation-state actors
- AI-driven automated attacks

### Key Threats
| Threat | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Ransomware | Colonial Pipeline attack |
| Phishing/Social Engineering | Tricks users into revealing info |
| Supply Chain | SolarWinds attack |
| APTs | Long-term nation-state sponsored attacks |
| Insider Threats | Authorized individuals compromising security |

### Best Defense Practices
- Regular risk assessments
- Zero-trust architecture
- Ongoing employee training
- Backup and recovery plans
- Continuous network monitoring

## Key Takeaways
- IT risk has 4 categories: Operational, Security, Compliance, Strategic
- Risk categories are interconnected — one breach triggers multiple risks
- ISO 31000 = flexible framework, NIST RMF = strict compliance framework
- Risk register tracks risk, likelihood, impact, mitigation, and owner
- Maersk NotPetya attack = $300 million lesson in risk management failure
- Zero-trust architecture is now a standard defense recommendation
- SOC analysts directly support risk management through detection and response
